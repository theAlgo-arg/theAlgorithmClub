How We Protect Information

The Algorithm Club implements reasonable administrative, technical, organizational, and operational measures designed to protect information against unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, misuse, loss, destruction, or other security risks.

  • Access Controls: Access to information is restricted to individuals, personnel, contractors, service providers, or authorized representatives who reasonably require access to perform their duties or provide requested services.
  • Secure Storage: Information may be stored using secure databases, cloud infrastructure, hosting services, backup systems, file storage systems, and related technology services selected according to business, technical, security, and operational requirements.
  • Redundancy & Backups: Information may be stored across multiple systems, backup locations, disaster recovery systems, or archived storage systems to reduce the risk of accidental loss, corruption, destruction, or service interruption.
  • Security Monitoring: Reasonable efforts may be made to monitor systems, detect unauthorized access attempts, investigate security incidents, and maintain the security of services and infrastructure.
  • Service Providers: The Algorithm Club may utilize third-party providers that maintain their own security, operational, and compliance practices to support the delivery of services.

While The Algorithm Club takes reasonable steps to protect information, no method of storage, transmission, processing, or electronic communication can be guaranteed to be completely secure.

The Algorithm Club cannot guarantee absolute security and shall not be responsible for security incidents, cyberattacks, unauthorized access, infrastructure failures, third-party service failures, or other events beyond its reasonable control.
